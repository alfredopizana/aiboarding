# New Hire FAQ

Owner: People Ops (Ana Torres, @anat). The questions every new hire asks in week one —
so you don't have to wonder whether it's okay to ask. (It's always okay to ask.)

## Week one

**When do my accounts arrive?** Everything (Google, Slack, Okta, GitHub) is provisioned
before day one — check the welcome email. Missing something? #it-helpdesk, it's a
same-half-day fix per the IT Runbook.

**Who is my onboarding buddy?** Named in your welcome email and your 90-day plan.
Buddies are the "no question too small" channel; they volunteered for exactly that.

**What should I actually do in week one?** Your 90-day success plan lists it, but the honest
summary: set up your environment, meet the people in your plan, ship something tiny, and
read your team's runbook. Nobody expects output in week one; we expect questions.

**Is it okay that I don't understand the acronyms?** Yes — the Glossary exists because
everyone before you asked too. Add the ones you had to ask about; it's a welcome first PR.

## Working here

**What are core hours?** There are none — we're async-first across 10 time zones. Your team
may have a sync window (ask your manager). Meetings cluster 14:00–17:00 UTC; written
Friday updates are the one hard rhythm. See Communication Practices.

**Can I work from anywhere?** Within your employment country, yes. Cross-border stints
over 2 weeks need People Ops sign-off (tax reasons, not trust reasons) — ask Ana early.

**How do I take time off?** Request in the HR portal, tell your team channel, done.
No approval theater for reasonable requests; managers only push back on coverage conflicts.
Minimum vacation is real — Ana chases people who *don't* take it.

**When is payday?** Last business day of the month, local payroll. Questions → #people-ops
(payroll questions are never "too small").

## Engineering-specific

**When can I deploy to production?** As soon as your PR merges — `main` auto-deploys to
staging and rides the next release train. Your buddy walks your first one with you.

**When do I join on-call?** After 90 days, shadowing first — never cold. See Incident Management.

**I think I broke something.** Say so immediately in the team channel — speed of honesty is
the whole game, and blameless is policy, not a poster. The person who reports their own
mistake fast is doing the most respected thing in our engineering culture.

**Where do I ask coding questions?** #dev-help for anything, your team channel for team
things. The rule of thumb: struggle for 30 focused minutes, then ask with what you tried.

## Money & benefits

**Equipment budget?** Standard laptop + peripherals from IT; anything beyond standard needs
your manager (see the RACI matrix, "Hardware beyond standard issue").

**Learning budget?** Yes — courses, books, conferences via People Ops; approval is your
manager's, receipts go in the HR portal.

**Health / benefits enrollment deadlines?** Country-specific; the HR portal shows yours.
When in doubt, ask #people-ops before the week-two enrollment cutoff.
