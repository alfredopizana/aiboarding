# Communication Practices

Owner: Operations (Ana Torres, @anat). Applies to Slack, email, docs, and meetings.
Default: async-first, written, and public — we span 10 time zones on purpose.

## Choosing the channel

- **Slack public channel** — default for everything. If it's about work, it's in a channel.
- **Slack DM** — logistics and personal topics only. Work decisions made in DMs get
  re-posted to the channel by whoever made them ("decision washing-up").
- **Email** — external parties, HR/legal matters, anything contractual.
- **Doc (Notion/spec)** — anything with a shelf life > 1 week or an audience > 1 team.
- **Meeting** — high-bandwidth topics: conflict, ambiguity, brainstorming, 1:1s. Not status.
- **Incident channel** — anything urgent and customer-impacting. Urgency never travels by DM.

## Async etiquette

- **No hello-only messages.** Put the question in the first message, with context and what
  you need ("answer by Thu" / "FYI only" / "blocking me"). nohello.net is company canon.
- **Response expectations**: channels 24 h, direct mentions same working day, DMs are not pagers.
  Urgent + customer-impacting → #incidents, everything else can wait for time zones to rotate.
- **Threads** keep channels readable; broadcast the conclusion back to the channel with "TL;DR".
- **Status updates are written**, in the team channel, before Friday EOD — three lines:
  done / next / blocked. Standup meetings are optional per team; written updates are not.

## Writing that respects the reader

1. Lead with the ask or the answer; background goes below, not above.
2. One topic per message or doc. Two topics = two threads.
3. Absolute dates ("by Thu Jul 23, 18:00 UTC"), never "EOD" across 10 time zones.
4. Name the decision-maker when you open a discussion, per the RACI matrix.
5. Disagreement is welcome in public and in writing — see Kind Candor in the values doc.
   Sarcasm doesn't survive text; don't ship it.

## Meetings, when we hold them

- An agenda in the invite or decline is fair game. Notes + decisions posted to the channel within 24 h.
- Default 25/50 minutes, cameras optional, recorded for the time zones that slept.
- Meetings between 14:00–17:00 UTC reach the most time zones fairly; rotate the pain for the rest.

## Language

Company language is English for anything cross-team; local-language channels
(#es-general, #pt-general) are encouraged for everything else. Write for non-native readers:
short sentences, no idioms doing load-bearing work. See the Inclusive Language guide.
