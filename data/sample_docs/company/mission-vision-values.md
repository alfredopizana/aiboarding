# Mission, Vision & Values

Owner: Leadership. Reviewed yearly at the January offsite; last review January 2026.

## Mission

Help every team turn their scattered knowledge into answers people can actually use.

## Vision

A workplace where nobody loses their first month hunting for the document, the owner,
or the acronym — where the company's knowledge works as hard as its people do.

## Values

These are decision-making tools, not posters. When two values conflict, say which one you're
trading off and why — that sentence belongs in the PR, the spec, or the decision log.

### 1. Customers before comfort

We do the inconvenient thing when it's right for the customer: the honest changelog,
the refund without a fight, the 2 a.m. rollback. Internal convenience never outranks customer trust.

### 2. Show your work

Decisions come with reasoning attached. Write things down where others can find them;
disagree in the open; cite your sources. A conclusion nobody can retrace is an opinion.

### 3. Small steps, shipped

Momentum beats magnitude. We ship the thin slice, learn, and ship again — in code
(trunk-based development), in product (weekly releases), and in process (pilot before policy).

### 4. Kind candor

We tell people the truth, and we tell it like we plan to keep working together.
Feedback goes to the person, not about them. Kindness without candor is neglect;
candor without kindness is cruelty. See the Communication Practices guide.

### 5. Own the outcome

"Not my job" is not in our vocabulary; "I'll find out who owns this" is. When something
breaks, we fix the system that allowed it (see: blameless postmortems), and when something
works, the credit is shared as widely as the work was.

## How values show up in practice

- Hiring: every loop scores one value-based interview; we hire for value fit, not culture sameness.
- Reviews: the growth conversation covers *what* was delivered and *how* — both count.
- Recognition: the monthly all-hands "values shout-outs" are peer-nominated in #kudos.
